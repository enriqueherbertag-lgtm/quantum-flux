# Quantum-Flux / Tenebra Link

**Cables get cut. We don't.**

## The Problem
Submarine cables are the weakest link in global connectivity. In 2024-2026 alone, dozens of critical cables have been severed in the Baltic Sea, Red Sea, and around Taiwan — many under suspicious circumstances involving anchors, ships, or geopolitical tensions. Governments, banks, and critical infrastructure can no longer rely solely on vulnerable undersea fiber.

## The Solution
Quantum-Flux / Tenebra Link is an ultra-resilient intercontinental backup link that operates completely independent of submarine cables.

- Combines **10 parallel streams** over existing commercial satellites (Starlink LEO + Hispasat, Intelsat, and others)
- An advanced **AI engine** intelligently fragments the data, distributes it across the streams with forward error correction (FEC), and seamlessly reassembles it at the destination

## Key Advantage
**If 2 or 3 streams fail** due to weather, technical issues, or sabotage — the link continues without data loss or interruption.

## Benefits
- Extreme resilience against climate, sabotage, or technical failures
- No new infrastructure required — we lease existing satellite capacity
- Rapid deployment (weeks, not years)
- Acceptable latency when continuity is the priority

## Business Model
We sell dedicated, guaranteed-resilient bandwidth as a monthly service to customers who cannot afford any downtime.

**Target Customers:**
- Governments & Defense (sovereign communications)
- Banks and financial institutions (high-value transactions)
- Critical infrastructure (energy, mining, data centers)
